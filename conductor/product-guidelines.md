# Product Guidelines

## Tone & Style
- **Senior Engineer Concise:** Prioritize technical clarity, brevity, and high-signal information.
- **Direct & Professional:** Avoid conversational filler; communicate intent and results directly.
- **No Mechanical Narration:** Focus on the "why" and "what," not just the "how."

## UX Principles
- **Intuitive & Discoverable:** Features should be self-explanatory, minimizing the need for external documentation for core workflows.
- **Transparent & Informative:** Provide clear, real-time feedback and status updates for all long-running or critical operations.
- **Action-Oriented:** The UI and CLI should guide users toward their next logical step in the infrastructure lifecycle.

## Design Aesthetic (Tailwind CSS)
- **Modern & Polished:** Leverage Tailwind CSS for a professional, clean, and responsive interface.
- **High-Signal Visualization:** Use visual elements (like the Topology Map) to simplify complex system states.
- **Consistency:** Maintain a unified layout, color palette, and interaction pattern throughout the platform.

## Naming Conventions
- **Semantic & Explicit:** Use clear, descriptive names for all infrastructure components, environments, and services.
- **Standard Industry Terms:** Align with established DevOps and cloud terminology where possible for familiarity.
- **Context-Aware:** Ensure names are unambiguous within the broader project and organizational context.
